01 / PROJECT OVERVIEW

One target, tested with several lines of evidence.

This project focuses on KRT16. The analysis will first establish its expression pattern, then test whether that pattern is connected to survival or clinical characteristics in the TCGA-LUAD cohort.

?

RESEARCH QUESTION

Is KRT16 altered in LUAD, and does its expression have clinical relevance?

I will compare KRT16 RNA expression between tumour and normal tissue, evaluate patient survival, and examine associations with available clinicopathological variables.

◎

WHY IT MATTERS

A convincing target story needs more than one significant plot.

Expression, effect size, survival, and clinical context answer different questions. Together they show whether KRT16 is simply detectable or is worth deeper biological investigation.

!

A work in progress: KRT16 is a research target, not a validated biomarker. Associations in this retrospective cohort will not establish causation or clinical utility.

05 / PRESENTATION CHECKLIST

What every result slide should contain.

Use the same cohort definition throughout and make each caption understandable without reading the analysis code.

  1. 01

    Start with the cohort

    State the TCGA project, data type, inclusion rules, tumour/normal counts, number of unique patients, and missing-data handling.

  2. 02

    Label the statistics

    Name the test and expression scale; report n, effect size, 95% confidence interval, and exact p-value beside each main result.

  3. 03

    Keep survival reproducible

    Define the endpoint and cutoff before showing the curve. Include censoring, numbers at risk, events, follow-up context, and Cox model details.

  4. 04

    End with a restrained conclusion

    Separate association from causation, acknowledge retrospective TCGA and multiple-testing limitations, and propose independent-cohort validation.

06 / REFLECTION

What I have learned so far.

01

Define the cohort before testing KRT16.

Sample type, patient duplication, missing outcomes, and normalization choices all affect what the comparison can support.

02

Effect size matters alongside significance.

A p-value alone does not describe the magnitude, direction, precision, or practical importance of a KRT16 association.

03

Validation is part of the conclusion.

TCGA can generate evidence for a hypothesis, but an independent cohort and biological experiments are needed before stronger claims.

“My goal is not to make every KRT16 result significant; it is to make every analysis clear, reproducible, and interpretable.”
