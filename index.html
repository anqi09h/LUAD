01 / PROJECT OVERVIEW

Learning by working with real cancer genomics data.

This project is my introduction to computational cancer research. I am learning how raw public data can be transformed into clear, reproducible evidence through code.

?

RESEARCH QUESTION

What mutation patterns appear in TCGA lung adenocarcinoma samples?

I am exploring which genes are frequently mutated, what types of nucleotide changes occur, and how these patterns could guide later comparisons with RNA expression.

◎

WHY IT MATTERS

LUAD is common, complex, and shaped by genomic changes.

Lung adenocarcinoma is a major subtype of lung cancer. Studying its molecular data helps researchers describe tumour biology and form better questions for future investigation.

!

A work in progress: This website documents my current process, not a finished scientific study. Results may change as I learn, check my code, and continue the analysis.

ENVIRONMENT

Mutation data

Simple Nucleotide Variation data is prepared as MAF data, then loaded with read.maf() for summary and visualization.

Expression data

Gene expression quantification is collected for Primary Tumor and Solid Tissue Normal samples, then organized into an expression matrix.

Current scope

The workflow currently focuses on data access, quality checks, mutation summaries, and expression matrix preparation.

05 / WHAT COMES NEXT

The analysis is only at its starting point.

My next goal is to move from data preparation toward a careful comparison of tumour and normal gene expression.

  1. 01

    Check the expression matrix

    Review sample labels, missing values, and expression distributions before analysis.

  2. 02

    Explore tumour vs. normal expression

    Learn an appropriate differential expression workflow and visualize broad patterns.

  3. 03

    Connect mutation and expression findings

    Investigate whether selected genes from the mutation analysis are also interesting at the RNA level.

  4. 04

    Document limitations

    Record methodological choices, possible sources of bias, and questions that remain unanswered.
